Science

Research

Our research sits at the intersection of quantitative genetics, plant genomics, and computational biology. We develop and apply cutting-edge approaches to understand how genetic variation shapes phenotypic diversity in plants — with a focus on traits relevant to crop improvement.

01
Quantitative Genetics

We study the genetic basis of complex, polygenic traits — characteristics controlled by many genes working together with the environment. Using genome-wide association studies (GWAS), QTL mapping, and genomic selection models, we identify the genetic loci and pathways that drive variation in traits like yield, drought tolerance, and nutrient use efficiency. Our work spans both model and crop species, with a strong emphasis on translational applications in plant breeding.

GWAS QTL Mapping Genomic Selection Heritability Mixed Models
🧬
🌱
02
Plant Genomics

We leverage advances in sequencing and genomics to understand gene function, population structure, and evolutionary history in crop plants. By analyzing large-scale genomic datasets — including SNP arrays, whole-genome sequencing, and transcriptomics — we explore how genetic diversity is structured across populations and how it can be harnessed for crop improvement. We are particularly interested in the genomic basis of local adaptation and stress tolerance in major crops like maize, soybean, and sorghum.

Population Genomics Comparative Genomics SNP Analysis Transcriptomics
03
Computational Methods

A core part of our work involves developing and applying bioinformatics pipelines and statistical models to handle the large datasets generated by modern genomics experiments. We use R, Python, and high-performance computing to build reproducible workflows for data analysis, visualization, and interpretation. We also explore machine learning approaches for genomic prediction and image-based phenotyping.

Bioinformatics Machine Learning R / Python HPC Data Visualization

Active Work

Projects

Our lab pursues a focused set of research projects that address fundamental and applied questions in plant genetics and genomics.

Active
Genetic architecture of drought tolerance in maize

Using multi-environment GWAS and genomic prediction to identify genetic variants and loci associated with drought stress response across diverse maize populations.

GWAS Maize Drought QTL
Active
Genomic selection for yield improvement in sorghum

Building and validating genomic prediction models for key yield traits in sorghum, with the goal of accelerating breeding cycles in dryland environments of the Great Plains.

Genomic Selection Sorghum Yield Great Plains
Active
Population genomics of soybean adaptation

Investigating how genetic diversity is distributed across global soybean germplasm and identifying genomic signatures of local adaptation using population genetics approaches.

Population Genomics Soybean Local Adaptation
New
Machine learning for image-based phenotyping

Developing deep learning pipelines to extract quantitative traits from field and greenhouse images, enabling high-throughput phenotyping for genetics studies at SDSU.
